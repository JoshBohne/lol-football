# LoL Football — Design Context

A daily puzzle site for League of Legends fans, themed around football roster building. Trading-card collectible aesthetic with esports broadcast polish.

## Design Context

### Users
LoL hardcore fans — players who watch Worlds, know champion lore, recognize Heimerdinger as Bandle City but hometown Piltover. They use the site between queues, after a stream, or in the morning with coffee. They expect Riot-broadcast visual quality and a sharp, considered interface that respects the source material.

### Brand Personality
**Tactile · mythic · sharp.** The product feels like opening a foil-stamped Panini sticker pack of Runeterra champions. Confident headlines, ornate hairline rules, broadcast-grade numerics. It is *not* a generic Wordle clone with a League skin — it's its own thing.

Voice: declarative, knowing, never hand-holdy. Copy treats the user as someone who already speaks the dialect.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Trading card / collectible draft.** Reference points:
- Panini sticker album spreads (asymmetric, ornament-edged)
- Magic: The Gathering / Hearthstone card framing (foil rules, embossed plate)
- LCS / Worlds broadcast lower-thirds (sharp tabular numerics, tight-tracked caps)
- The Athletic / Sports Illustrated editorial layouts (confident type hierarchy, generous whitespace)

**Anti-references**: NYT Wordle minimalism, Discord-bro neon, generic shadcn dashboard, gradient-text marketing pages, video-gamey HUD chrome.

**Theme**: Dark. Deep midnight navy with gold-foil accents. Tinted toward warm so it reads like card stock under low light, not a generic dark-mode dashboard.

### Design Principles
1. **Ornament earns its place.** Hairline gold rules, tracked caps eyebrows, and tabular numerics convey card-collectible texture without resorting to drop shadows or glassmorphism.
2. **Asymmetry over center-everything.** Left-aligned headlines with generous trailing space. Stats and metadata sit in the margins like a card back, not centered placards.
3. **Typography carries the weight.** A condensed display face for broadcast headlines, a wide grotesk body for legibility. Tabular numerics everywhere a number lives.
4. **Champions are the heroes.** Every layout choice should let champion portraits breathe. The chrome is a frame, not a competing surface.
5. **Motion is restrained and tactile.** Micro-tilt on hover, foil-flash on lock-in, staggered card reveal on submit. No bounce, no spring elastic, no looping animations.
